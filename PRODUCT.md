# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two first-class audiences, plus one adjacent:

1. **Sellers / consignors** — lenders, captive finance companies, credit unions, and fleets
   deciding who remarkets their repossessed and off-lease vehicles. They care about days-to-sell,
   recovery vs. MMR, claims, and transport cost. They are evaluating Stream as a vendor, often in a
   compliance-sensitive, RFP-style process. The site must read as credible to a bank's vendor team.
2. **Buyers / dealers** — independent and franchise dealers sourcing inventory. They want to *see
   what's running* fast: scan the Run List, filter, find units, and know where each one sells.
3. **Title-services clients** — lenders/dealers needing fast, multi-state title work (a distinct
   Stream offering, currently a separate site at streamtitleservices.com).

## Product Purpose

Rebuild **thestream.com** for **Stream Remarketing Services**, the remarketing arm of United
Recovery & Remarketing (sibling of UAX). Stream's pitch is omnichannel reach with end-to-end
control: one consignment listed in-lane and online across every major channel, managed from the
repo yard to liquidation. The site replaces a thin Squarespace template and must (a) win
consignments from sellers and (b) serve buyers a genuinely useful, live Run List. Success = the site
makes Stream look like the most capable, most modern remarketer in the category, and converts both
audiences to contact / browse. Tagline: **"Inlane. Online. Everywhere."**

## Brand Personality

**Confident, networked, precise.** Modern and tech-forward, built around the hub-and-spoke network
idea (one seller, every channel). Crisp data over adjectives; quiet dominance over stat-dumping.
Underneath runs the United-family warmth (Memphis, the "Uniteder" culture, causes), but Stream is
the networked, digital-first sibling, not the BBQ-forward one. Voice: direct, declarative, frictionless
("anywhere, any time"). No em dashes site-wide; "and" over "&" in body copy.

## Anti-references

- **The old Squarespace site** (the explicit one to beat): thin content, low-res tiles, no real
  hierarchy, obvious template. Every page must feel authored, not assembled.
- Generic SaaS/startup landing: cream backgrounds, gradient-blob heroes, identical icon-card grids,
  a tiny tracked eyebrow above every section.
- Stuffy corporate-bank: stock handshake photos, navy-and-gray sludge, lifeless legal tone.

## Design Principles

1. **Everywhere is the proof.** The omnichannel claim must be *shown* (the hub-and-spoke network,
   the live Run List, named channels), never just asserted.
2. **Two front doors.** Seller pitch and buyer tool are both first-class from the hero down; neither
   is buried.
3. **Data is the credibility.** Real, live inventory and real channels do more persuading than
   adjectives. Lead with the working Run List, not a screenshot of one.
4. **Quiet dominance (breadth over specifics).** Lead on capability, reach, and since-2008 history;
   keep exact volumes and named clients off the public site (reserve for sales).
5. **Family backbone, Stream voice.** Inherit the proven UAX/UR2 conventions and the shared blue,
   but let Stream be the precise, networked, modern sibling.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (watch lime on light, and muted gray on tinted surfaces);
large text ≥3:1. Tap targets ≥44px. Every animation needs a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative.
Color is never the only signal (status pills carry text, not just hue). Mobile-audited at 375px min.
